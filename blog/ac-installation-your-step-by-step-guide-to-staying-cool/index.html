AC Installation: Your Step-by-Step Guide to Staying Cool

When the summer heat arrives, a reliable air conditioning system is more than a luxury it’s a necessity. Whether you’re upgrading an old unit or installing a brand-new system...

Salmon HVAC
Published October 9, 2025  •  Updated October 9, 2025  •  5 min read

When the summer heat arrives, a reliable air conditioning system is more than a luxury it’s a necessity. Whether you’re upgrading an old unit or installing a brand-new system, understanding the process can make all the difference in comfort and efficiency. This guide walks you through every step of AC installation, offering practical advice and expert tips to help you make the best choices for your home. With Salmon HVAC’s experience and commitment to quality, you’ll be well-equipped to enjoy a cool, comfortable indoor environment all season long.

Assessing Your Home’s Cooling Needs

Every home is unique, and so are its cooling requirements. Start by measuring the square footage of your living space. This number is crucial, as it determines the size and power of the AC unit you’ll need. Generally, you’ll want about 20 BTUs per square foot, but factors like ceiling height, insulation, and window placement can influence this calculation.

If your home features open layouts, vaulted ceilings, or multiple stories, these elements can affect how air circulates. Older homes with less insulation may require a more powerful system, while newer, energy-efficient homes might get by with a smaller unit. Take note of any areas that tend to get hotter than others—these “hot spots” may need extra attention during installation.

Choosing the Right Air Conditioning System

Selecting the right AC system is about more than just size. Central air conditioning is ideal for cooling an entire home, especially if you already have ductwork in place. These systems provide consistent temperatures throughout the house and are often more energy-efficient for larger spaces.

Ductless mini-split systems, on the other hand, offer flexibility. They’re perfect for homes without existing ducts or for cooling specific rooms. Each unit operates independently, allowing you to control temperatures in different zones. This can lead to significant energy savings, especially if you spend most of your time in just a few rooms.

When comparing systems, pay close attention to the SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) rating. Higher SEER ratings mean better efficiency and lower energy bills. Investing in a high-efficiency unit may cost more upfront, but the long-term savings are worth it.

Preparing for Installation

Before installation begins, take time to prepare your home. Clear the area where the outdoor unit will sit, ensuring at least two feet of space on all sides for proper airflow. Check your electrical panel to confirm it can handle the additional load of a new AC system.

If you’re installing a central system, inspect your ductwork for leaks or damage. Sealing any gaps with mastic or specialized tape will improve efficiency and prevent cool air from escaping. Clean your vents and make sure your insulation is in good condition these small steps can have a big impact on performance.

The Installation Process

The actual installation involves several key steps. First, choose the best locations for both the indoor and outdoor units. The outdoor unit should be placed on a level surface, away from direct sunlight if possible. The indoor unit needs to be positioned for optimal airflow, usually in a central location.

Next, install the mounting plate for the indoor unit and secure it firmly. Connect the refrigerant lines and electrical wiring, making sure all connections are tight and properly insulated. Run the condensate drain line to direct moisture outside, and double-check that everything is sealed and secure.

Once the units are in place, it’s time to power up the system. Test the thermostat, check airflow from the vents, and monitor the refrigerant pressure. If everything is working as it should, you’ll notice a difference almost immediately—a cool, comfortable home ready to take on the hottest days.

Common Installation Challenges

Even with careful planning, challenges can arise. One of the most common issues is choosing the wrong size unit. An undersized system will struggle to keep your home cool, while an oversized unit can lead to humidity problems and higher energy bills. Always use a manual J calculation or consult with a professional to determine the right size.

Improperly sealed ductwork is another frequent problem. Leaks can waste energy and reduce cooling efficiency. Take the time to inspect and seal all ducts before installation. Electrical issues can also occur, so make sure your wiring is compatible with the new system and meets all safety standards.

If you run into problems you can’t solve, don’t hesitate to call in the experts. Salmon HVAC’s team is trained to handle even the most complex installations, ensuring your system is set up safely and efficiently.

FAQ

How do I know what size AC unit I need?

Calculate your home’s square footage and consider factors like ceiling height, insulation, and window placement. For the most accurate results, consult with a professional who can perform a manual J calculation.

Do I need a permit to install a new AC system?

Most areas require a permit for AC installation. Check with your local building department or ask Salmon HVAC for guidance on the necessary paperwork.

How long does installation take?

A typical installation takes between four and eight hours, depending on the complexity of the job and any modifications needed.

What maintenance does my new AC system need?

Change filters every one to three months, clean coils annually, and schedule regular professional inspections to keep your system running efficiently.

Can I install an AC system myself?

While some homeowners tackle installation on their own, professional installation is recommended for safety, efficiency, and compliance with local codes.
