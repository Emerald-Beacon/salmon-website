Why Commercial HVAC Is Different—and Why Experience Matters

While residential systems prioritize comfort and aesthetics, commercial HVAC systems are engineered for performance, scale, and regulatory compliance. They often involve:

  • Complex zoning and ductwork
  • Larger-scale units and rooftop systems
  • Advanced automation and energy controls
  • Strict indoor air quality (IAQ) requirements
  • Business continuity concerns

These systems require technicians who not only understand the technical side but can also work around operational hours, adhere to codes, and communicate clearly with facility managers and business owners.

That’s exactly what Salmon HVAC delivers—commercial-grade service, backed by local experience.
