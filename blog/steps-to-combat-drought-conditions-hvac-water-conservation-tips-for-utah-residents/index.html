Steps to Combat Drought Conditions: HVAC Water Conservation Tips for Utah Residents

Salmon HVAC
Published June 26, 2025  •  Updated June 23, 2025  •  6 min read

Drought conditions can significantly impact daily life in Utah, where water scarcity is increasingly common during the summer months. Adjusting your HVAC system and adopting mindful, water-saving practices can help you stay comfortable while supporting essential conservation efforts. At Salmon HVAC, we believe that everyone can make a difference—and we’re here to show you how. In this guide, we’ll share practical HVAC-focused tips to reduce water waste and maximize efficiency, all while contributing to a more sustainable future in Northern Utah.

Recognizing the Signs of Water Scarcity in Utah

Understand Drought Indicators

Wilting plants, dry soils, and lower levels in Utah’s reservoirs are visible indicators of drought. Reduced snowpack and minimal rainfall are common triggers for these conditions. When your lawn browns quickly or new watering restrictions are announced, these are your cues to take action.

Drought also affects local ecosystems—increased pest activity and altered animal behavior (like more deer sightings in urban areas) often signal declining water availability. Pay attention to local news and weather updates for real-time drought levels and restrictions.

Follow Climate Trends

Utah has experienced hotter, drier summers in recent years. By tracking historical rainfall data and average temperatures, you can prepare your household for conservation measures. A dry forecast means it’s time to optimize your home for efficiency and reduce reliance on water-heavy systems.

HVAC Efficiency: A Key to Water Conservation

Upgrade to Low-Water HVAC Systems

Modern HVAC technology includes systems that use less water and operate more efficiently. Evaporative coolers, while effective in dry climates like Salt Lake, must be chosen carefully to avoid unnecessary water consumption. Opt for ENERGY STAR-rated air conditioners that rely less on water-intensive energy production.

Smart thermostats with humidity sensors also help regulate moisture and temperature efficiently. By reducing overuse, they limit the demand on water-cooled systems and help lower your overall environmental impact.

Maintenance to Prevent Water Waste

Just like regular tune-ups improve energy efficiency, they also prevent water waste. During inspections, Salmon HVAC technicians check for:

  • Leaky water coils or cracked pipes
  • Blocked or clogged condensate drain lines
  • Poor insulation around ducts and joints

These small issues can lead to big water losses over time. Replacing old filters, sealing air leaks, and insulating refrigerant lines ensure your system works smarter, not harder. Preventative maintenance can cut HVAC water usage by up to 20% over the season.

Water-Smart Cooling Alternatives for Utah Homes

Embrace Passive Cooling

Reduce dependence on mechanical systems by using passive cooling strategies:

  • Open windows during early mornings and evenings
  • Create cross-ventilation with window fans
  • Install reflective window films or solar shades

These methods minimize your HVAC system’s load, helping reduce both energy and water use. Adding indoor plants that require minimal watering can improve air quality and temperature control without taxing your water budget.

Use Thermostat Technology Wisely

Smart thermostats are vital in managing water-efficient HVAC operations. They adjust based on your schedule and can even respond to outdoor humidity and temperature. Features like geofencing and remote access ensure your system isn’t running unnecessarily.

Choose models that track usage trends and alert you to inefficiencies. Over time, these insights help you refine your usage habits for maximum comfort and conservation.

Outdoor Practices that Complement HVAC Efficiency

Xeriscaping Around HVAC Units

Planting drought-resistant landscaping like lavender, sedum, or ornamental grasses around your outdoor HVAC unit shades the system and reduces ambient heat. Less heat means less energy and water used to cool your home.

Be sure to:

  • Keep plants trimmed 2–3 feet from the unit
  • Use mulch to retain soil moisture
  • Avoid overwatering by using drip irrigation

This creates an eco-friendly landscape that supports your HVAC system’s performance without draining your water supply.

Reuse Water with Graywater Systems

Graywater systems collect lightly used water from showers and laundry for irrigation. While not directly connected to HVAC, reducing your home’s overall water demand supports conservation goals. Use biodegradable products and avoid diverting graywater into HVAC-related drains unless approved by a professional.

For advanced options, consider pairing graywater systems with smart irrigation controllers to automate and optimize outdoor water use based on weather conditions.

Building a Community of Conservation

Educate and Empower Your Neighborhood

Sharing HVAC maintenance tips and water-saving strategies can multiply your impact. Host workshops, post on local forums, or distribute flyers in your HOA to spread awareness. Topics like "How HVAC Systems Affect Water Usage" or "Seasonal Maintenance for Drought Prevention" can inspire your neighbors to act.

Join Local Water Initiatives

Utah municipalities often host water-wise campaigns or rebate programs for smart thermostats and HVAC upgrades. Get involved with conservation groups or city-led drought planning meetings. These platforms offer resources and amplify community-wide water-saving efforts.

Find rebates and conservation programs at SlowTheFlow.org or through your city’s water department.

Why HVAC Conservation Matters in Utah

Utah ranks among the fastest-growing states in the U.S., placing strain on limited water resources. HVAC systems, especially when not maintained, can waste significant water through leaks and inefficiencies. Every homeowner has a role to play in reducing that burden.

By adopting smarter cooling strategies, prioritizing maintenance, and educating others, you contribute to a resilient, water-smart Utah. These efforts protect the environment, preserve community water supplies, and lower your utility bills.

Conclusion: Cool Homes, Conserved Water

Combatting drought doesn’t require dramatic sacrifices. With HVAC-focused conservation strategies, Northern Utah residents can enjoy comfort and sustainability. From upgrading to water-smart systems to routine tune-ups and community advocacy, small changes lead to big savings.

Take the first step by booking a maintenance check or installing a smart thermostat. Then, share your journey—because when communities come together to conserve, everyone benefits.

FAQ

Q: How do drought conditions affect HVAC systems?
A: Drought can reduce water availability for systems that rely on water-based cooling or energy generation. It also affects air quality and humidity, making HVAC systems work harder.

Q: What HVAC upgrades help conserve water?
A: Upgrading to energy-efficient units, installing smart thermostats, and switching to air-cooled over water-cooled systems can all reduce water usage.

Q: How often should I maintain my HVAC system during a drought?
A: Twice a year is recommended, especially before summer. Look for signs like leaks, clogged drains, or increased humidity indoors.

Q: Can landscaping help reduce HVAC water waste?
A: Yes. Xeriscaping reduces heat around the outdoor unit and conserves water through native, drought-resistant plants.

Q: Where can I find water conservation rebates for HVAC upgrades?
A: Visit salmonhvac.com or your local city utility website for current offers in Northern Utah.
